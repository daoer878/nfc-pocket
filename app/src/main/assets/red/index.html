    图书馆地点和开放时间

    太古汇2座26楼图书馆
    16:00 – 18:00 on every Monday (Exclude Public Holiday) 16:00 -18:00每周一 (不包括节假日)

    如何借书

    1. 在Book² 预订想要借书籍
    2. 选择取书时间
    3. 在取书时间到达图书馆取走预订的书籍

    如何还书

    请在借阅到期之前将书籍归还到图书馆。

    注意事项

    a. 每位同事每次只可以预订并借走一本书；
    b. 对于未按时取书的预订，预订记录将会保留一周，之后系统会自动取消这个记录；
    c. 同事可以在Book² 上自助取消所预订的书籍；
    d. 书籍的借阅期限为一个月；
    e. 如果想获取更长的借阅时间，可以在Book² 上进行续借，续借期为一个月，每本书只能续借一次。
    f. 请保持图书清洁及完整，如有损坏或遗失，需购买新书或以相等金额作为赔偿。

    联系方法

    如果你有任何疑问, 欢迎向培训部同事咨询:
    电邮: Technical Development China GLT
    电话: Technical Training CN

    注意事项
    在享用膳食之余，请各位同事也配合做好如下的事项:
    1.  请在餐厅用餐。餐厅的所有餐具，例如：杯子，盘子或碗是不能带到办公区域的。在办公区域用餐会产生不利他人的气味以及容易滋生细菌，甚至导致鼠泛，危害员工健康。为了保持办公室内的空气清新，请勿携带有浓重气味之食物如榴莲、豆腐乳、咸鱼等。
    2.  请正确使用楼层内的冰箱，请勿连同饭盒袋放进冰箱存放，自备的食物请用保鲜袋或者干净的塑料袋包装好并摆放整齐，以保持冰箱的整洁。每个周末，冰箱内所有物品会被清理并消毒，请同事在周六前取回自己的物品。
    3.  自携午（晚）餐的同事请自备餐具，不要使用提供给在餐厅就餐同事使用的餐具(包括筷子、勺子、纸巾等)，以免影响其他同事的正常用餐；
    4.  请勿擅自将餐厅内的餐具（包括杯子、碟子、刀叉、纸巾等）及食品拿到办公楼层；如有特殊活动需求（如颁奖/开张典礼，大型会议等），请经部门经理批核后发至CRE FM Helpdesk与餐厅协商再具体安排，但要求尽量避免正常就餐高峰时段；
    5.  自携午（晚）餐的同事请将就餐后的剩余饭菜或外卖饭盒等，倒入餐厅指定的垃圾桶内不要在洗手间及茶水间内清洗自带饭盒以免造成洗手盆排水堵塞及虫害；
    6.  请勿随意借用餐厅餐台或餐椅，以免影响其他同事的正常用餐，如因特殊需求，请向CRE FM Helpdesk申请，并按规定时间归还；
    7.  凡会议、比赛或庆祝活动需租用餐厅场地，请至少提前一周联系CRE FM Helpdesk @ *505 或发邮件到CRE FM Helpdesk GZC/HDPG/HSBC(GZC), CRE FM Helpdesk TKH China GSC GLT/HDPG/HSBC(TKH), CRE FM Helpdesk NHC China GSC/HDPG/HSBC(NHC)具体安排，但要求尽量避免正常就餐高峰时段(11:00-14:00)。

    Overviews Certifications

    集团年销售额超过100亿英镑，业务遍及60多个国家，员工人数超过50万，每年供餐超过60亿份。
    FTSE100(伦敦证券交易市场指标股)成员之一。
    我们为客户提供高品质的全方位的餐饮服务、餐饮服务解决方案及前期筹备方案。
    康帕斯集团于1997年进入中国，并使用自有品牌"怡乐食"在中国大陆地区开展业务。
    目前康帕斯在中国大陆地区已经拥有超过300个运营项目，服务区域覆盖至50多个大中城市。
    独立的HSE部门
    由持有国家级证书的专业质检人员负责完成供应商审核，食品原材料抽检，现场卫生安全环境评估等工作，完全独立于各部门之外直接向大中华地区总裁汇报，以保证对食品生产流通各个环节进行有效的监督。

    IJP Policy and FAQ

    China (HSDC) - Internal Job Posting (IJP) Policy and FAQ

    Policy

    • Introduction

      HSDC is committed to the development of its employees and providing them with opportunities to acquire new experience and competencies, and enhancing their career and growth potential through internal job moves.

      This provides opportunity for career advancement and development as an employee takes on different roles that are more complex or broader in scope.

    • Eligibility

      All permanent employees of HSDC meeting the eligibility criteria as defined under this policy can apply for IJP:

      • Employees who have completed 12 calendar months of regular full time employment in current designation and department are eligible to apply to roles through IJP dependent upon satisfactory performance at the last annual review and passing applicable competency assessment (wherever applicable).
      • In cases where an employee received an 'Inconsistent Performer' rating for performance or an 'Unacceptable' rating for behaviours and is seeking a role change, it is recommended that the employee, current entity/functional manager and hiring manager engage in dialogue to understand the full performance history and why a role change is appropriate for both the employee and the organisation. Where an individual does not have a performance rating, their current line manager should endorse any applications. Any employee who has received an 'Unacceptable' rating for behaviours and is under-performing in their current role should be under performance review and should not be able to apply for roles.
    • Guidelines
      • Fulfilment of positions will be based on suitability of desired skills and on a first-come, first-served basis
      • Selection will be governed by HSDC hiring process
      • Employee and hiring manager cannot revisit a decision once the release dates are finalised
    • Procedures
      • The resourcing team will publish open positions on CareerLink for employees to view and apply.
      • Employees with the desired skills, experience and readiness to take on more complex and/or roles that are broader in scope at the current or next level can apply for an open position.
      • The employee is to advise the current manager before applying for any IJP.
      • Once employees have applied for a position, the resourcing team will screen them for suitability and further processed to the hiring manager for short listing, based on the desired skills and expertise required for the published position
      • Employees short-listed for positions must undertake a formal interview process with the hiring manager
      • The hiring manager will share feedback with the resourcing team, who in turn communicate to the employee.
      • If the employee is selected for an opportunity, the acceptance / refusal has to be communicated in writing within five working days of selection confirmation, failing which the opportunity will be offered to the next selected candidate
      • Default release dates are in line with notice periods by country. If the employee is working on a critically time-sensitive project, negotiation on the release date should be conducted between the release manager and the hiring manager to reach an agreement. The longest extension of release date is up to three months after the offer acceptance date
    • Roles and responsibilities
      • Employee
        1. 1) Apply for job positions relevant to their experience and aspirations through CareerLink
        2. 2) Ensure all the project handover formalities are completed before the decided last working day
        3. 3) Inform of application to current manager before applying for any IJP
      • Hiring manager
        1. 1) Plan for resources from within the organisation with defined skills and domain.
        2. 2) Shortlist applicants based on the requirement.
        3. 3) Prompt feedback to the resourcing team on the status of applicants during the selection process.
        4. 4) Agree with the releasing manager for the date of release.
        5. 5) Discuss future expectations with aspirant, set new performance objectives and sign off the new job description when necessary (e.g. when the work stream is changed).
        6. 6) Counsel / Advise the applicant on future opportunities.
        7. 7) Prepare a training plan based on the profile of the selected applicants.
      • Releasing Manager
        1. 1) Counsel / Advise the applicant on future opportunities in the current team (wherever applicable).
        2. 2) Provide all facts and data to support the IJP selection decision.
        3. 3) Highlight the stakeholders about the following:
          • - Resignees and PIP Cases
          • - Maternity cases
          • - Complete the employee’s performance appraisal
        4. 4) Plan a smooth release of the employee from the current assignment.
        5. 5) Notify HR on the release date for successful applications.
        6. 6) Ensure the releasing of the employee within one month upon the offer acceptance date. Any extension of release date due to exceptional cases should be agreed with hiring manager with support of business need approved by delivery head / department head wherever applicable.
      • Department Heads / Delivery Heads / Recruitment Heads
        1. 1) For any concerns on the release dates the delivery / department head of the concerned releasing manager must discuss the business need with HR.
      • Human Resources
        1. 1) Screen the application against eligibility and propose the shortlisted applicant to the hiring manager.
        2. 2) Facilitate interview process and prompt response to the stakeholders.
        3. 3) Notify applicants and related parties on the interview results.
        4. 4) Keep all stakeholders informed about the movement plan including movement effective day.
        5. 5) Arrange negotiation between the releasing team and new team for any exceptional release date cases.
        6. 6) Facilitate the release and joining of the employee to new role with an approved position.
        7. 7) Ensure timely management of the process.
        8. 8) Guide and counsel the applicant with reference to the new role / opportunity along with the hiring manager.
        9. 9) Ensure clearance movement workflow with update in HR systems.

    Internal Job Application FAQ

    • 1.How can I apply for an internal job opportunity?

      A: Employee can find internal vacancies on CareerLink. Interested employee should go through internal application procedures. Before you submit any application, you should notify your current manager accordingly to the IJP policy.

    • 2.What do I need to know about applying for internal transfer?

      A: Before applying for any internal job, please go through the details of eligibility of Internal Job Posting (IJP) Policy to see if you are eligible for the application. All the internal job application should be done through CareerLink.

    • 3.What's the procedure for IJP Advertisement?
      • A: 1) Make sure approved headcount is ready;
      • 2) Complete Hiring Request Form;
      • 3) Send posting request to respective Resourcing Partners.
    • 4. I was promoted in past 1 year, can I apply internal job?

      A: According Internal Job Posting policy, you have to work in same team / same role for at least 12 months. If you have promotion record in last 12 months, we are so regretful to inform you that your application is Not eligible at this moment.

    Quick Start Guide - CareerLink

    • Accessing CareerLink

      1. Click in HRDirect.

      2. In the Preferences section, select Privacy Settings.

      3. Update your profile opt-in/out status which indicates your option to have your employee data transferred/not transferred to CareerLink. Please do remember to click “Save” in the bottom of the page.

    • Search and apply for jobs

      1. Log in CareerLink via HRDirect homepage

      2. On the Job Search page, select "Information Technology" in the job field and select ‘Asia Pacific -> China -> Guangdong -> Guangzhou’ or ‘Asia Pacific -> China -> Shanxi -> Xi’an’ in the job location, and then click "Search for Jobs" or directly input a requisition ID in the field of Job Number.

      3. You will then see a list of jobs. To see the details of a specific job, click its title.

      4. To apply for a specific job, click the Apply Online button.

      5. You will then be taken to complete sections of information for your application.

      When finished each section, click Save and Continue to go to the next. On the Summary page, click Submitto complete the process.

    Internal Job Application in CareerLink FAQ

    • 1. I am having difficulties uploading my CV / Resume to my candidate profile

      A: If you are encountering difficulties uploading your CV / Resume first check that you are using the correctformat. CV / Resumes must be saved in Word, PDF or Excel format and be smaller than 2048kb. If you arestill unable to upload your CV / Resume, please select "I do not want to upload a CV / Resume", click“Save and continue” and add your CV / resume as an attachment..

      If you continue to experience difficulties, please raise a case via HR Direct, with subject “CareerLink –Failed to upload CV”.

    • 2. How do I withdraw an application for a role?

      A: To withdraw your application log into CareerLink and click on the tab titled 'My Job Page'. The summaryof your job application includes a link to 'withdraw' your application.

    • 3. Can I re-apply for a role if I have previously withdrawn from the process?

      A: You can re-apply for a role that is still available by clicking on the re-apply link on the My Jobpage.

    • 4. How can I check the status of my job application?

      A: To view the status of your job application, log into CareerLink and click on the 'My Jobpage' tab. Eachof your active job applications will display the date you applied for the role and your current status.

    • 5. Can the information supplied on an application be modified?

      Some information supplied on an application can be modified via CareerLink by accessing yourcandidate profile and clicking 'Edit'. This will enable you to amend the necessary information.

      Please note, personal information such as name, contact details, current role and performance ratings isuploaded from GHRS and where appropriate, can only be updated via the 'Personal Information Summary'link on My HR Helpdesk.

    • 6. Can I apply for a role with HSBC Group on the external job board?

      A: No. You should only apply for roles via HRDirect portal to CareerLink.

    • 7. I have forgotten my password or my account is locked. What should I do?

      A: If you have forgotten your CareerLink password, go to the login page and click on the 'forgot yourpassword' link. Follow the instructions and your password will be sent to you by email. If you are locked outof your CareerLink account please raise a case via HR Direct.
